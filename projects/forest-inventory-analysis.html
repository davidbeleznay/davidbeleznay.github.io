Climate-Smart Forestry

Leveraging AI to advance adaptation and mitigation strategies in working forests

The Crucial Role of Working Forests in Climate Action

Forests represent one of our most powerful allies in addressing climate change—both through mitigation (carbon sequestration) and adaptation (building resilience in our ecosystems). Drawing from my experience leading climate initiatives and building extensive monitoring networks, I'm passionate about combining AI technologies with forestry expertise to accelerate our progress toward climate-smart forestry practices.

"The forests are borrowed from my children, and it is a great challenge for us all to reduce our emissions and ensure a resilient forest that can sustain the climate our future foresters will be living in."

As the recipient of the Forest Professionals BC's 2023 Climate Change Innovator Award, I've dedicated my career to developing practical solutions that help working forests maximize their climate potential while remaining economically viable. Now, I'm focused on how AI can transform our ability to measure, monitor, and manage forests for optimal climate outcomes.

AI-Powered Climate Solutions for Forests

Advanced Carbon Monitoring

AI systems can now process complex satellite and LiDAR data to provide much more accurate carbon stock assessments across large forest landscapes. These tools allow us to move beyond traditional inventory methods to track carbon in real-time and model future scenarios under different management regimes.

Natural Asset Valuation

The true value of forests extends far beyond timber—they provide clean water, biodiversity habitat, and carbon sequestration. AI helps quantify these ecosystem services, enabling more holistic management decisions and creating new revenue streams for forest owners through carbon markets and other mechanisms.

Adaptation Modeling

Climate change is already affecting forest health and productivity. By analyzing vast datasets from weather stations, soil sensors, and tree monitoring systems, AI can help predict how different forest types will respond to changing conditions and identify the most resilient management practices.

Regeneration Optimization

The forests we plant today will grow in a different climate than we've experienced historically. AI can analyze complex climate models alongside site-specific data to recommend the best species and genetic varieties for long-term forest health and carbon sequestration.

Case Study: BigCoast Forest Climate Initiative

I led the groundwork for Mosaic's BigCoast Forest Climate Initiative, a program that enables companies to achieve their carbon footprint goals more rapidly by investing in improved forest management. This initiative represents the intersection of forest economics, climate science, and practical forestry—a perfect application area for AI tools that can:

  • Improve measurement accuracy - Using AI to analyze remote sensing data, we can provide more precise carbon accounting at lower costs
  • Model scenarios - AI helps us predict how different management regimes will affect carbon sequestration rates over decades
  • Optimize monitoring networks - I oversaw a network of 36 satellite-linked weather stations; AI now helps us determine optimal placement and data integration
  • Generate compelling visualizations - Complex carbon data becomes accessible through AI-powered visualization tools that communicate the climate benefits of forests

The AI Opportunity: Bringing Natural Asset Stories Forward

There's a tremendous opportunity to use AI to amplify the stories and case studies that demonstrate the value of natural assets. Working forests are often overlooked in climate discussions, yet they represent some of our most powerful climate solutions when managed properly.

Data Integration

AI systems can integrate diverse forest data—from LiDAR and satellite imagery to ground-level measurements and historical records—creating a comprehensive picture of forest carbon dynamics that would be impossible to assemble manually.

Scenario Modeling

By processing thousands of variables, AI can model how different forest management approaches will affect carbon sequestration, biodiversity, water quality, and timber production—helping foresters make optimal decisions for multiple values.

Communication Tools

One of AI's most powerful applications is in translating complex forest data into compelling visuals and narratives that clearly communicate the climate benefits of working forests to stakeholders, investors, and the public.

Monitoring & Verification

For carbon markets to function effectively, we need cost-effective, reliable methods to verify carbon benefits. AI dramatically reduces the cost and improves the accuracy of monitoring forest carbon stocks at scale.
