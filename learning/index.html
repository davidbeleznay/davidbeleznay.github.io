AI Forestry Glossary

Essential terms and concepts at the intersection of AI and forestry

AI & Machine Learning Terms

Artificial Intelligence (AI):

Computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, reasoning, and problem-solving.

Machine Learning (ML):

A subset of AI that enables computers to learn and improve from experience without being explicitly programmed for each task.

Neural Network:

A computing system inspired by biological neural networks, used to recognize patterns and make predictions from data.

Natural Language Processing (NLP):

AI technology that helps computers understand, interpret, and generate human language in a meaningful way.

Forestry AI Applications

Computer Vision:

AI technology that enables machines to interpret and analyze visual information from images or video, used for species identification and forest monitoring.

Remote Sensing:

The acquisition of information about forests using satellites, aircraft, or drones, often combined with AI for automated analysis.

Predictive Modeling:

Using AI to forecast future forest conditions, growth rates, or environmental changes based on historical and current data.

Digital Twin:

A virtual representation of a forest system that uses real-time data and AI to simulate and predict forest behavior.

Data & Tools

Training Data:

The dataset used to teach an AI model how to perform specific tasks, crucial for developing accurate forestry AI applications.

API (Application Programming Interface):

A set of protocols that allows different software applications to communicate, often used to integrate AI services into forestry systems.

Cloud Computing:

Delivery of computing services over the internet, enabling access to powerful AI tools without local hardware requirements.

Prompt Engineering:

The practice of crafting effective instructions for AI language models to get the best results for forestry-specific tasks.
